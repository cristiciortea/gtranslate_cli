When you tell a joke to a peasant he laughs thrice, the first time he laughs when you tell him the joke; the second time when you explain the joke; and the third time when he understands the joke.
A noble laughs twice. One time he laughs, when you tell him and a second time, when you explain it to him. Comprehensionally speaking, he won’t get it anyway.
An officer laughs only once, at the time you tell him, for he won’t let it be explained and as for understanding he won’t.
A Jew, when you tell him a joke, goes "I know I know, old jokes" and (thinks) he can tell the joke better .
